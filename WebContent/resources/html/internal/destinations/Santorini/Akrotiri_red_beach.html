Akrotiri - Red Beach.

After roaming around Oia and spectating easter celebrations at Pyrgos , the second day was allocated for Akrotiri,red beach and the sunset at Oia that was missed on the first day.

Akrotiri , I was told to have ancient excavations remains dating back to thousand of years old.The excavations at Akrotiri have uncovered one of the most important prehistoric settlements of the Aegean.Its quite far from Fira town but since I had a bike it did not seem that difficult reaching the spot.In the dedicated parking lot you will be charged to park your vehicle and the entrance by itself costs 15 euros.I had so much of expectations to see what was in stake.

The place has well preserved remains by archealogists who have done extensive research in unearthing this place.The entire place is covered under the roof and you are not supposed to go near most of the ruins.It was highly disappointing that they had only few very remains for the display for an hefty charge at the entrance.

On the flip side , red beach located just few hundred metres is one of the best hunted spots in Santorini.The mountains resemble red color due to the high volcanic activity remains from the past.People here ask us to refrain from swimming due to frequent rock falls.However , you will miss an enthralled experience if you do not take the risk.

Most of the people who come here swim in the less rough tides and hence you will not be afraid of.I chose to get into the water and was so thankful that my belongings were taken care of by a Spanish couple who were taking sun bath.I was in the water for almost one and half hours.It was such a soothing and relaxing atmosphere to relish.
