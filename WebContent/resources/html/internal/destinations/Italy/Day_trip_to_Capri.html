Naples - Capri day trip.

The most exaggerated and high price island near Naples is Capri.I had Capri as part of my itinerary mainly due to Blue Grotto or Blue Caves.I could not visit one in Vis Island , Split when I was there due to bad weather.Fortunatelty , when I was in Naples the weather was favourable.Another advantage of Capri blue caves is that it is operational till 5.30 unlike the one in Croatia where they close it by 1 or 2 PM.

Started in 9.30 ferry from Municipo ( the station name the same if you travel in line M1). After reaching the island I book the full island tour that includes visit to Blue grotto for 18 euros that doesn't include entrance cost of 13 euros.

They island tour last for one hour and covers northern southern eastern and western parts showing you the best parts of the island. They start with place where a fisherman icon is place on top of the rock. Then they show you the arch shaped rocks the green caves light house peak points of the cliffs the caves the two big rocks and finally blue grotto. The waiting time to Blue grotto at the entrance can be painful if the crowd is huge. On the day I went it was almost two hours but all was worth the wait. You are shifted into a tiny rowing boat where no more than four people are seated.

The guide asks you to sit deep inside since the opening to the caves is very very small. I met another solo traveller en route and we decided to capture photos of each other. The entry to the caves itself is quite breath taking and the color of the waters inside is simply stunning that you can never imagine as to how it would have looked from outside. You get only five to ten mins inside to stay but it's all the worth and it's one unique experience.
