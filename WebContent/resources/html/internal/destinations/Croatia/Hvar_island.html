HVAR island day trip from Split.

I was supposed to do blue caves myself by making it to vis island but unfortunately the weather was not supposed and the tour was cancelled. However I had alternate plans of HVAR or BRAC and finally chose HVAR. The choice was never a disappointment and HVAR is such a a beautiful place to spend. Did a day trip from split by taking jadrolinija catamaran.

Took a 9.45 one and reached around 11. The first place that everyone visits is the castle standing a tall a proficient landmark in the tiny island. The walk is just 15mins to the top and the view is majestic. You get panaromic view of the island from the top. A drink in the sun sitting there getting an aerial view is soothing and relaxing. The second half of the trip was towards a secluded island near HVAR.

There are two islands out of which you can choose to spend. I chose less crowded one and the to and fro journey is just 40kunas.people go there just to relax and swim in the less rough seas. I spent one and half hour in the water. It's soo cool to swim in the sea at ease with sea remaining calm throughout the day.The beach here was a nude one with most of people stripped off clothes and were relazing under the sun.

Ensure you take a sandal to walk along the rocks. There is a restaurant in each of the island incase if you Don take your own food. I carried my lunch.

I had an hour and half waiting time for our ferry and I was relaxing. When the time came I approached the boarding point and on my way met a couple again whom I met in the morning during my castle walk we were delighted and started interacting more on each one's whereabouts I was explaining my solo travels and they were so impressed. They also confessed that they have quit their jobs and have planned to travel the world as much as possible . They were from Aruba a Dutch colony in carribean island.

I requested them for currency and coin of their country since I am a numismatist and currency collector. They gave me a currency and coin each for which I paid an equivalent exchange. I resisted to accept it as their gift since the value was quite high. The coin was square shaped and came to know the only country in the world to mint square coins. Another best memory of solo travelling.
