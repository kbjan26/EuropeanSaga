Anne Frank House - Heineiken Beer Factory.

The only museum of its kind which will always hold a special place in my heart. This was included in my itinerary as part of my visit along with my friend and was recommended by a person living in Amsterdam.

We reached this place around 9.30 AM in the morning hoping to get the entry and exiting early so that we could continue our journey.To our dismay , we had to wait for almost four hours in the queue.This impatient waiting time made us read the pamplet that was served outside the house in multiple versions.We grabbed a english version and started reading through the content unwillingly.

I was intrigued by the contents of the passage since I did not collect prior knowledge of the house.On a candid front , I must admit that to have really been moved upon reading and listening to the stories and suffering of this lady. She had survived with many of her familiy members concealded inside a room staying mum all during the day with little opportunity for much interaction during the night for survival during Hitler regime.

The wait was really worth and visit to this place will have a definite hangover for atleast few mins after you come out.

Heinieken beer factory has nothing much apart from the display as to how beer is made. Small demo of the entire life cycle is available inside the house with complementary beer served at the end.However , one this that will amaze is the brand value that it has got all over the globe and the place it has stamped in this arena.
