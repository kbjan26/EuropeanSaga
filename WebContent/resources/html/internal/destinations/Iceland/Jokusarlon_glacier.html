Jokusarlon Glacier.

Day trip to Jokusarlon glacier is probably the longest day trips of all.Its is located in the South eastern corner and is almost 400 Kms away.We started around 7 AM in the morning and what was in store were magnificient landscapes.

Enroute to this beauty includes stop at Vik village,Skogafoss waterfall,black sand beach.

The guide was pretty experienced and briefed us on the rich Icelandic history.The population of whole Iceland is not even more than half a million.The enroute spots included farms where home grown agro products were briefed about.We were also shown schools in remote places where education style was completely different from the contemporary world.

After a brief journey , we stopped at black sand beach which was literally filled with black pebbles and surrounded by black hills due to high volcanic activity in the past.It was truly an amazing spot to visit.Then , we headed to a nearby village called Vik for lunch and refreshment.The tiny village we were told has less than 300 inhabitants.

Post noon travel was to the actual destination, Jokusarlon.Its the largest glacier lake in Iceland.After almost 5 hours of travel we finally reached.The view of the large ice cakes were stunning and we could see large group of seals which is a primary inhabitant in this region.It also started raining by the time we reached this spot and hence could not spend much time outside.There is also a cafe in this region supplying refreshments.

On our return fortunately the sun started shining and the enroute stop was at skogafoss waterfalls.Skogafoss is one of the largeest waterfalls in Iceland which gives you a stunning view from the roads.We had almost an hour.This place was already covered in my itineray as part of Solheimajokull hike.However,it was sunny this time around

In order to compensate for the rainfall we had at sunshine which had just over taken the rainfall. We were welcomed with a bright ray of rainbow which fell over the waterfall.Gradually this thine ray doubled to showcase what the nature had in reserves.No wonder why nature is supreme.I had some of the best shots captured at this place.
