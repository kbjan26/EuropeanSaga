La Tomatina- BUNOL - Spain.

La Tomatina - the most cherished,most celebrated,most awaited of all my solo saga.Probably the only event I was waiting for so much time and planned several months prior and the one I did not want to miss.

Tomatina is a traditional world famous festival where people gather from all around the globe to meet to have the experience and fun of throwing tomatoes at each other. It is celebrated on the last Wednesday of August every year in the town of Bunol near Valencia Spain. The event happens between 11AM and 12PM leaving spell bounded and amazed in the end. I was hoping for a good fun but never knew lifetime experience was on cards.

Brief history of La Tomatina.

La Tomatina or the tomato festival is a Spanish festivity celebrated in the town of Bunyol, Region of Valencia at the end of August, typically the last Wednesday of the month. The origin of the tradition of La Tomatina dates back to August 1945. During the Giants and Big-Heads figures parade taking place at the town square several young people from the crowd wanted to take part in the parade, in spite of the objection displayed by the participants. This turn of events caused disorder, resulting in one of the participants getting knocked down, who in a fit of rage went to the nearest stall -which happened to be a market stall of fruit and vegetables- and grabbed some tomatoes. And thus chaos ensued, with the entire crowd throwing tomatoes at each other, until the local law enforcement intervened in this battle of vegetables and put an end to it.

One year later, on the same day, the local young people tried to improvise a tomato throwing festival by starting a quarrel themselves, except this time they brought their own tomatoes from home. This went on for several years, gaining considerable popularity and becoming a tradition of sorts. However, during the 1950s, La Tomatina was temporarily banned, which of course didn't stop the tomato festival from happening, resulting in many young people getting imprisoned. La Tomatina became an official festivity in 1957.

Mandatory Checklist :

  1. A waterproof cover for the phone.
  2. A waterproof bag to keep the phone further secured.
  3. Waterproof shoes /slippers.
  4. Swimming goggles is a must.
  5. Carry enough madness along.

After quite a delay, started around 3AM and reached bunol around 8PM. You are made to gather as a group after which t-shirt and wrist band are issued to the participants. You can leave your luggage inside the bus and also you have lockers near the entrance.

Once you cross the entrance, you are made to assemble in the permissible lanes. The crowd is too much that you cannot even move and you move along with push from all sides. Initially the localities watching the event from the house secured pour water and throw small tomatoes to energize crowd.

The atmosphere and momentum gets huge that you start going crazy. Then the giant trucks March towards the crowds and from then on events rolling out are unstoppable and unimaginable. Kudos to the organiser taking utmost safety and precautions for the event. Enough volunteers regulate the gathering when trucks start coming in.

Initially my thought were that we will be given a glimpse of tomato throwing and will be made return back. To my amusement it was not the case and there were 4 huge trucks that followed one after the other leaving the place heavily flooded. The crowds go crazy and start smashing pouring tomatoes at each other leaving you drenched and wet completely. You do not have time to even think what's going around and plethora of happiness are on cards.

Ensure you take enough photos during the event so that the memories are captured for lifetime. Tiny little town offers fun to thousands of people in a short span of time.
